# Luna and the Light Garden

## Story Bible (for illustration consistency)

**Main Character:** Luna — a small girl, about 5 years old, with curly dark hair tied with a bright yellow ribbon, wearing a blue dress with white polka dots and red rain boots. Round face, big brown eyes, warm brown skin.

**Setting:** A grey, rainy village that transforms into color through Luna's garden. Simple cottages with stone walls. Rolling green hills in the background.

**Art Style:** Soft watercolor children's book illustration. Warm palette with muted backgrounds and pops of bright color on Luna and her flowers. Gentle, rounded shapes. Soft lighting. No harsh shadows. Style of Oliver Jeffers meets Beatrix Potter.

**Color Progression:** Pages 1-3 are muted greys and blues (the rainy village). Pages 4-6 introduce greens and one bright color (the first sprout). Pages 7-9 become increasingly colorful. Pages 10-12 are full, vibrant color (the garden in bloom).

---

## Page 1

Luna lived in a village where the rain never stopped.

It pattered on rooftops. It drummed on windows. It turned every puddle into a tiny grey sea.

## Page 2

The other children stayed inside, faces pressed against foggy glass.

But Luna pulled on her red boots, buttoned up her coat, and splashed straight out the door.

## Page 3

"Why do you go out in the rain?" asked her grandmother, every single morning.

"Because the rain is trying to tell me something," said Luna. "I just haven't figured out what yet."

## Page 4

One morning, Luna found a seed in a puddle. It was gold, no bigger than her thumbnail, and it hummed — just a little — when she held it in her palm.

She knelt in the mud behind the cottage and pressed it gently into the earth.

## Page 5

The next day, a tiny green sprout pushed up through the grey soil.

Luna crouched down close. The sprout had one perfect leaf, and on that leaf sat a single raindrop that glowed like a lantern.

## Page 6

She whispered to it every morning. She sang to it in the afternoons. She told it stories at twilight — stories about sunshine she had only seen in books.

And the sprout grew, and grew, and grew.

## Page 7

By the end of the week, a flower opened — the brightest orange Luna had ever seen, like a tiny sun planted in the ground.

The rain kept falling. But wherever the flower's light touched, color seeped back into the world. The grey fence turned green. The brown path blushed pink.

## Page 8

Luna planted more seeds — in every crack, every corner, every forgotten patch of mud in the village.

Stems climbed up lampposts. Buds burst open along stone walls. Vines curled around doorframes like friendly arms welcoming people home.

## Page 9

The other children pressed their faces to their windows and gasped.

Then, one by one, they pulled on their boots and came outside.

## Page 10

Together, they planted and watered, sang and whispered, until the whole village bloomed.

Red poppies on the rooftops. Purple wisteria dripping from every eave. Yellow sunflowers standing tall as the children themselves.

## Page 11

It still rained. It rained every single day.

But now the rain fed the flowers, and the flowers fed the color, and the color fed something warm inside every person who walked through the village.

## Page 12

Luna's grandmother watched from the doorway, smiling.

"You figured it out, didn't you?" she said. "What the rain was trying to tell you."

Luna looked up, rain on her cheeks, flowers at her feet, the whole village bright around her.

"It was saying: *everything you need to grow is already here.*"
